**Content:**
- **Objection Philosophy:**
  - Objections mean they're engaged
  - Every objection is a question in disguise
  - No objections might mean no interest

- **The Objection Handling Framework:**
  1. **Listen** fully without interrupting
  2. **Acknowledge** their concern
  3. **Clarify** what they really mean
  4. **Respond** with empathy and evidence
  5. **Confirm** you've addressed it

- **The Big 5 Objections (and Responses):**

  1. **"Your price is too high"**
     - What they're really saying
     - Reframing around value and ROI
     - When to negotiate vs. stand firm

  2. **"I need to think about it"**
     - Uncovering the real concern
     - Time-based vs. doubt-based hesitation
     - Moving forward without pressure

  3. **"We're already working with someone"**
     - Current provider satisfaction assessment
     - Planting seeds for future opportunity
     - Differentiation strategies

  4. **"I need to talk to [spouse/partner/boss]"**
     - Identifying the real decision-maker
     - Multi-stakeholder selling
     - Offering to be part of that conversation

  5. **"This isn't a good time"**
     - Timing vs. priority
     - Budget cycle questions
     - Staying top of mind

- **Objection vs. Condition:**
  - Objections can be overcome
  - Conditions are deal-breakers
  - Knowing when to walk away
